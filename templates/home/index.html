F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why Auctions?

    Auctions are exciting, a far better way to sell your property than the current available methods. Its quick, your property can be sold in 30 days or less. The auction platform is also the only platform where you will be able to get more money for your property than you expected because you put a reserve on the value of the property with the assistance of your Business Development Manager. Auctions work with motivated buyers and sellers and provide the market with true market value of a property at that given time.

  • Why Online?

    Online auctions opens up your buyers to anyone in the world who is looking to buy. It also allows you to bid from anywhere in the world or the country you may be in so you don’t lose out on an a property you wanted because you not available to attend an auction.

  • Am I protected when I buy on Auction?

    All companies selling property (real estate)in South Africa need to be registered with the EAAB (Estate Agency Affairs Board) who are the regulator and such have to be in possession of a current Fidelity Fund certificate. While there is an industry body called SAIA (South African Institute of Auctioneers), Auction Boys does not belong to them. This does not mean Auction Boys is an illegal auctioneer, we choose not to belong to a body that cannot regulate. Auction Boys was established in 2005 all our auction comply with the Consumer Protection Act of 2008.

  • Can Buyers make offer before the Auction?

    Absolutely, auction is a sale method and nothing else, while the prevailing sales method is private treaty, auctions offer the market another option to sell. Both sellers and buyers want the same thing, “A quick sale at the best price”.

  • What do I need to Bid at an Auction? Registration and Deposit

    In order to bid on auction, you need to be registered with your FICA documents (ID & Proof of Residence), and approved. Approved basically means that we have gotten a pre-approval for you to buy meaning you will be able to afford the property. You will need to pay a deposit which may vary according to the value of the property you bidding on. The deposit is fully refundable should you not be successful.

  • Default Auction

    Should you bid on auction and win the property but are not able to perform, pay for the property, it is known as a default sale. The seller, in terms of the Auction Rules, will now take legal action against you, the auctioneer can on the sellers instructions, re-auction the property at your, the defaulting parties cost, and take whatever bid they get, you the defaulting party now being liable and responsible for the difference between the default bid and the new bid received do. Our strongest advice is to get prequalified and know you can afford to buy, this is another reason why we qualify you, we don’t want to put you, our sellers, and ourselves in that situation.

  • Reserve and Guide Prices

    Everything in life has a value, opinions on the value of the asset differs and that is the basis of an auction, most property sold on auction is sold with a reserve price, there are instances where there is no reserve and this is known as an absolute action. Examples are Sheriffs auctions, insolvent estate auctions, liquidation sales.

  • Voetstoets, “As Is Sale”

    Auctions are “voetstoets” sales, which means as is, as you see the property when you buy, that is what you buy. The seller has no obligation to you after the sale. This is why it is important to work with reputable auction houses and auctioneers and also to do your homework properly. By way of example, a Sheriffs auction, the buyer is responsible for all arrear rates and taxes and municipal accounts on the property, while you may buy a bargain, you may be hit with a large bill.

  • Sold Subject To Confirmation (STC)

    All property is sold on auction, where the reserve price is not reached, it is sold STC (Subject to Confirmation). The seller will have 5 business days to accept of decline the offer. During the conformation period you are bound to your offer and may not retract or withdraw your offer.

  • What do I need to Bid at an Auction? Registration and Deposit

    In order to bid on auction, you need to be registered with your FICA documents (ID & Proof of Residence), and approved. Approved basically means that we have gotten a pre-approval for you to buy meaning you will be able to afford the property. You will need to pay a deposit which may vary according to the value of the property you bidding on. The deposit is fully refundable should you not be successful.

  • Ownership, Risk and Transfer

    The auction rule will determine the terms and conditions of the sale, generally possession and risk is transferred to the buyer on registration of transfer in the buyers name at the deeds office. There are instances where the risk and ownership will be transferred on the fall of the hammer, be sure you understand and read the Auction Rules.
